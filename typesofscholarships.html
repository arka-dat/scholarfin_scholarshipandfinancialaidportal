SCHOLARSHIPS THAT WE SUPPORT

Scholarships are financial awards given to students to help them pay for their education. They can be based on various criteria, including academic achievement, financial need, extracurricular involvement, and specific talents or interests. Below is a comprehensive overview of different types of scholarships available to students.

1. Academic Scholarships

These scholarships are awarded based on a student's academic performance, such as GPA, standardized test scores, and overall academic excellence.

Examples:

  • Merit-Based Scholarships: Offered by colleges and universities to students with outstanding academic records.
  • National Merit Scholarship: Awarded to high-achieving students based on their PSAT/NMSQT scores.

2. Need-Based Scholarships

These scholarships are designed to assist students who demonstrate financial need. Eligibility is often determined by the FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid).

Examples:

  • Pell Grants: Federal grants for low-income undergraduate students.
  • State Grants: Various states offer need-based financial aid to residents attending college.

3. Athletic Scholarships

These scholarships are awarded to students who excel in sports. They are often offered by colleges and universities to recruit talented athletes.

Examples:

  • NCAA Scholarships: Offered to student-athletes participating in NCAA sports.
  • NAIA Scholarships: Available for athletes competing in the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics.

4. Talent-Based Scholarships

These scholarships are awarded to students who demonstrate exceptional talent in areas such as music, art, or performing arts.

Examples:

  • Art Scholarships: Offered by colleges for students pursuing degrees in visual arts.
  • Music Scholarships: Available for students with exceptional musical abilities.

5. Diversity Scholarships

These scholarships aim to promote diversity and inclusion within educational institutions. They are often awarded to underrepresented groups.

Examples:

  • Hispanic Scholarship Fund: Supports Hispanic students pursuing higher education.
  • United Negro College Fund (UNCF): Provides scholarships for students from underrepresented groups.

6. Community Service Scholarships

These scholarships are awarded to students who demonstrate a strong commitment to community service and volunteer work.

Examples:

  • Prudential Spirit of Community Awards: Recognizes students who have made a positive impact in their communities.
  • Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation: Supports students who have demonstrated a commitment to community service.

7. Military Scholarships

These scholarships are available to students who are serving or have served in the military, as well as their families.

Examples:

  • GI Bill: Provides education benefits to military personnel and veterans.
  • ROTC Scholarships: Offers scholarships to students who participate in the Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) program.

8. Employer Tuition Reimbursement

Some employers offer tuition reimbursement programs to help employees pay for their education.

Examples:

  • Google's Education Assistance Program: Offers tuition reimbursement to employees pursuing higher education.
  • Microsoft's Tuition Reimbursement Program: Provides financial assistance to employees pursuing degrees in technology-related fields.

9. Professional Association Scholarships

Many professional associations offer scholarships to students pursuing degrees in specific fields.

Examples:

  • American Medical Association (AMA) Scholarships: Offers scholarships to medical students.
  • National Association of Social Workers (NASW) Scholarships: Provides financial assistance to social work students.

10. Government Scholarships

The government offers various scholarships to students pursuing higher education.

Examples:

  • Federal Pell Grant: A need-based grant program for undergraduate students.
  • TEACH Grant: A grant program for students pursuing teaching careers.
